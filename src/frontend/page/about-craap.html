About MetaCheck and the CRAAP Framework

MetaCheck helps you evaluate the credibility of online information using key aspects of the CRAAP framework. CRAAP stands for:

  • Currency: The timeliness of the information
  • Relevance: How well the information fits your needs
  • Authority: The source of the information
  • Accuracy: The reliability and correctness of the content
  • Purpose: The reason the information exists

What MetaCheck focuses on

MetaCheck uses three core aspects of CRAAP to support your analysis:

  • Currency: Is the article or source up to date?
  • Authority: Who created or published the content? Are they credible?
  • Relevance: Does the information match your needs or context?

When you input a URL, MetaCheck extracts metadata related to these aspects. This includes:

  • Publication and modification dates
  • Author and publisher information
  • Keywords and descriptions
  • Persistent identifiers like DOIs

Why it matters

MetaCheck doesn’t replace human judgment — it supports it. By surfacing metadata clearly and transparently, it helps you:

  • Spot outdated or questionable sources
  • Understand who’s behind the content
  • Decide whether the source fits your purpose
